You now have both yardsticks: CALMS for culture and practice, DORA for outcomes.
Let us apply them to the stack that will be the living example of the whole
course. It is not a lab case: it is a real setup, with its strengths and its
debts.

## The current state

The reference stack: **dozens of NestJS microservices** running on **on-prem k3s**. CI
lives in **GitHub Actions** and builds each image to publish it to **GHCR**. The
deploy is **only `skaffold deploy`**, by hand, from the laptop of whoever
remembers. Secrets are **committed in git**, as literals inside kustomize's
`secretGenerator`. There is no **CD** (continuous delivery) and no **metrics** of
any kind.

## Audit it against CALMS

- **Culture** (partial): whoever writes the service also operates it, which is
  good. But operational knowledge is not shared.
- **Automation** (halfway): build and release are automated up to GHCR; the
  deploy is 100% manual. The chain is cut at the last mile.
- **Lean** (fragile): because deploying hurts (it is manual), you tend to batch
  many changes into each deploy. That is the opposite of small batches.
- **Measurement** (zero): without metrics you do not know your deployment
  frequency or your lead time. You are flying blind.
- **Sharing** (weak): "how this gets deployed" lives in one head, not in a
  runbook.

## Audit it against DORA

Without instrumentation, the four metrics are eyeball estimates: low deployment
frequency (whenever someone remembers), lead time of hours or days (commit →
manual skaffold), unknown change failure rate, and MTTR that depends on who is
available. The diagnosis is clear: medium-low speed, unmeasured stability.

## Three anti-patterns we will fix

1. **Mutable tags** (`:master`, `:development`): they point to an image that
   changes under your feet. Today's `:master` is not yesterday's, so you cannot
   reproduce or roll back with certainty to a concrete version.
2. **Secrets in git**: the keys inside `secretGenerator` stay in history forever.
   Anyone with access to the repo —or an old clone— has your production
   credentials.
3. **Manual deploy**: every hand-run `skaffold deploy` is an unaudited,
   non-reproducible, easy-to-forget step. It is the Automation hole and the
   reason you cannot measure anything.

## The course roadmap

Each module closes one of these gaps:

- **Module 3 (Docker)**: hardens the images —multi-stage, non-root user, small
  and reproducible images—.
- **Module 4 (CI/CD)**: fixes the mutable tags (you move to immutable
  per-commit-SHA tags) and completes CI with tests and reliable publishing.
- **Module 5 (Kubernetes + GitOps)**: adds real **CD** (automated deploy,
  goodbye to hand-run `skaffold`) and pulls **secrets** out of git with secure
  management.

By the end, this same stack will have gone from "automated CI + everything else
manual" to an automated, measurable, reproducible chain. The rest of the course
is that journey.
